Our Locations

Since our opening in 2013, Subedei has stayed on the cutting edge of historically-accurate world adventure cuisine. Let us treat you to a feast worthy of the great khans!

Named after Chingis Khan’s greatest general, Subedei brings together the flavors of China, India, Central Asia, Russia, Syria, Iran/Persia, Iraq, the Levant, Saudi Arabia, Hungary, Austria and Poland eloquently incorporated by the culinary traditions of twelfth-century Mongolian steppe culture.

The Mongol empire was not simply about the conquest and devastation of agrarian peoples by marauding nomads - it was also about cross-cultural exchange. The Mongols absorbed the arterial silk road between East and West and expanded it dramatically - what had been a single thread of communication became woven into a rich, elaborate trans-asiatic tapestry. Just as Alexander the Great’s conquests created the Hellenic world, Genghis Khan created the modern concept of Asia. Subedei restaurant articulates the culinary component of this cultural exchange for modern palates.

Our chef and founder

Dr. Jacques Temuchin (PhD. History, University of Texas, CV) is a leading practicioner of Historical gastronomy. A dashing explorer fluent in Uyghur, Jurchen, Medieval Russian, and Arabic, Temuchin is an expert in reconstructing medieval recipes with scrupulous attention to detail. He served as a sous chef at Medieval Times, Austin for two years.

“Dr. Jack” is also a captivating lecturer available for two-hour history slideshow/dinner/drinking parties upon request.
